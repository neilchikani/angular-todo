Verification of the encumberance certificate

Encumbrance Certificate is often the first document to be verified while purchasing property and usually helps the buyer to reject the property at initial stage itself, in case of any adverse findings. This document is verified by our experienced lawyer on current seller details, free flow of title between previous buyers and sellers, prior mortgage and if the mortgage is paid off, any present or prior litigations in the court of law etc. It is highly recommended for the buyer to obtain an encumbrance certificate from sub registrar office even if the seller gives you the original to avoid getting cheated
